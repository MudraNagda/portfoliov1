OVERVIEW

I had the previlege to design the website for DJ ICACTA 2020 - the 2nd International Conference of Advanced Computing Technologies and Applications - held at my college, Dwarkadas J Sanghvi College of Engineering.

DURATION

September - November, 2018 (Phase 1)

USER RESEARCH

My co-ordinators had surmised a set of guidelines and rules they wanted to follow for the website. I also went through the previous conference’s websites to create the appropriate information architecture.

AIMS

1. To ensure a hassle-free navigation across the website. The user should be able to swiftly find location, schedule, paper domains and the paper submission link.

2. To create a theme that resonates with the conference theme (Intelligent Computing) itself.

HIGH FIDELITY PROTOTYPE

Most of the design decisions were taken in meetings with the head of the Computer Engineering Department. I would then design a prototype and we would repeat the process after receiving feedback.

Few important points in the UI:
1. The landing page answers the all the “W-H” questions a site visitor may have - What is the conference about, What is the theme, When is it going to happen and Where will it be held. The page also communicates the theme “Intelligent Computing” through the graphic on the right.
2. Efficient navbar links for easiest navigation
3. The color scheme resonates with the theme. I chose a ‘motherboard green’ as the primary color and a shade of orange for highlights. After several meetings and iterations, we finalized the design. You can view the website here.

WHAT I LEARNT

1. It was interesting to think empathetically while designing something as technical as a Conference website. Most conference websites are cluttered and overcrowded with information, often following an old conventional format. I enjoyed the challenge of breaking this pattern by using a clean and neat layout instead.

2. My co-ordinators and supervisors were teachers who have seen a conventional web layout for conference websites. Convincing them to adopt a different approach took a little perseverance and effort. Luckily, they understood the need to do so and also suggested changes of the same nature.

3. Another important takeaway from this project was that I learnt over time to welcome changes suggested from the client (co-ordinators) and also to implement these changes as quickly as possible.
