// crimson //

Angry
you throw words like darts
aimlessly
But yet a miscalculated aim - to burst
with rage and fury
Your face is unrecognisable
scared people who love you
scared of people who love you
But you felt so alone so
you made her face unrecognisable
Now she can't see her self
or walk out without carrying the weight of sadness - your fury
They say anger makes you face consequences
unless you realise what your worth is

It's the face you look at in the glass
you were going to smash
The face your mother trusted
when you said you'd be her good boy
The face that your father holds pride in
when he saw you take that job
The face that you once saw in newspapers
but never for this reason
Yet you wonder why you feel cuffed
when lashing out felt like freedom -
hers, that you snatched
You feed off her self esteem - the one you never had
Now now, don't act so surprised, causing depression is crime in crimson.
.
.
