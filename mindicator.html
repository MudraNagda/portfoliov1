OVERVIEW

Spotting pain points and trying to redesign inconvenient human experiences from everyday life is something I find extremely enjoyable.
Commute is an essential part of our everyday routine. Having used Mumbai Local Trains for over two years now, I could not help but notice that I myself had gotten used to workarounds on the App’s UI - ignoring the time it took to navigate through the app.
So I took up the long overdue challenge to redesign the m-Indicator app.

USER RESEARCH

For this project, I did not require extensive research. The premise being that having used it for 2 years, I have collected enough pain points and could point out the exact flaws in the app’s experience.
However, I did take inputs from fellow users at several stages of the prototype development process.

AIMS

1. To ensure easy navigation and visibilty of all the features on the first screen - reduce the steps taken in navigation

2. To create a seamless experience while checking a train route from A to B (most frequently used feature)

3. To clearly display the names of stations, bus stops, platforms, etc

4. Redesign overall UI (while sticking to the color scheme) to soften the effect of red and black

HIGH FIDELITY PROTOTYPE

Before I started the low or high fidelity prototype, I went through the Material Design documentation for Designers. I redesigned the app entirely with Material Design in mind. Once I finalized the low fidelity prototype, I started working on colors, font, shadows etc. The decision to follow Material Design made it a lot easier to design the UI.

Some major changes I made from the previous version of the app are:
1. Categorizing the options on the home page (see fig 2)
2. Using a dynamic map visual where the user can entire source and destination stations and check train routes (see fig 2)
3. Dedicating a floating button to view the entire map
4. Designed a more accessible favourites button

WHAT I LEARNT

1. How helpful Material Design is! I gained a lot of insight from how Google made this design framework. It helped me understand the difference between Mobile UI and Web UI, which I have done a lot of before.

2. Redesigning an app the way I would use it was a very gratifying experience. However, I was cautious of not being trapped in my own sense of reality. To do so, I looked at different perspectives while solving an issue that is faced by not just me, but a thousand others.

3. Since this app is used by thousands of commuters daily, I wanted most of the design to seem “invisible”. So I decided to retain the earlier color scheme. This, although, was challenging, as red and black is a tough scheme to balance. It took time and effort to get it right, but it was a great experience.
