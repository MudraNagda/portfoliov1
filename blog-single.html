HIGH FIDELITY PROTOTYPE

Once the layout and structure was decided, I started working on the high fidelity prototype. My client had a basic color scheme in mind - red and dark blue. To make the website seem more kid friendly, I introduced yellow to the design.

One of the goals was to make the information as visual as possible, so I used a lot of icons and graphics.

Considering that an average person browses a website for 15s, we wanted to ensure maximum information absorption within this short time period. The solution to this was to have sections of solid color with clear text over it. Our aim was to minimize the textual content as much as possible and replace it with visuals but without creating a hodgepodge of images and graphics - which would in turn increase cognitive load for the user. For essential information points which could not entirely be replaced by a visual, we used a combination of text and visual. Webflow was very useful for animation and transitions - making it easier for the designer and the developer to show a trial of how each transition would look on the webpage.

After several meetings and iterations, we finalized the design. You can view the website here.

2. I paid attention to and listened to every point my client had. But, it would have been solely based on my perception had my client not shared stories and experiences of students and parents.
